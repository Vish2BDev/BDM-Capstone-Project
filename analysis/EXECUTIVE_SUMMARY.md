# Executive Summary
## Pure'O Naturals Branch Sales Optimization
### Data-Driven Inventory, Pricing & Margin Strategy

**Organization:** Pure'O Naturals, Branch 0007-ANJANEYA NAGER, Bangalore
**Period:** April 2025 – September 2025 · **Revenue:** ₹2.54 crores · **SKUs:** 3,247

---

## The Situation

Pure'O Naturals, a specialty organic retail chain, operates branch 0007-ANJANEYA NAGER in a high-traffic residential-commercial neighborhood. Despite generating ₹2.54 crores in 6-month revenue from 52,314 transactions, the branch faces **silent profitability leakage** across three dimensions: portfolio complexity, pricing inconsistency, and inventory inefficiency.

The branch owner's concerns — "too many products, inconsistent pricing, vegetable wastage" — signal systemic operational gaps that data analysis can quantify and resolve.

---

## The Analysis

This project applied **primary transactional data** (6 months of POS CSV exports) through a rigorous analytical pipeline:

- **Data:** 52,314 transactions · 3,247 SKUs · 183 days · cleaned to 99% accuracy
- **Methods:** ABC Classification · CV Volatility Analysis · Margin Proxy Modeling · Price Variance Index · X-MR Control Charts · Risk Stratification · DSLS Slow-Mover Detection

## The Strategy (3-Pillar Framework)

### Pillar 1 — SKU Portfolio Rationalization (12 weeks)
Reduce SKU base from 3,247 → 1,800 (44% reduction). Phase out 1,447 tail SKUs through staged liquidation and discontinuation. Result: Revenue per SKU +80%, purchasing overhead −15%.

### Pillar 2 — Pricing & Margin Optimization (8 weeks)
Reprice 869 below-floor SKUs using tier-based intervention (+2–5% price adjustments). Deploy centralized pricelist with override audit trail. Target: Portfolio margin 21.4% → 23.0%.

### Pillar 3 — Dynamic Inventory Management (8 weeks)
Replace fixed reorder points with demand-responsive ROP formula for 746 high-volatility SKUs. Implement 30-day slow-mover protocol for 97 dormant products. Target: Stockouts −68%, wastage −60%.
