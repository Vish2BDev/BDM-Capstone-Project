Data & Methodology

6 Months of Primary POS Data → Business Intelligence

📋

Primary Data Collection

6 monthly CSV exports from POS system (Apr–Sep 2025). Transaction-level granularity: date · product · quantity · unit price · total revenue. Formal authorization from branch owner.

🧹

Cleaning Pipeline

Normalization · missing value imputation (0.02% rows) · 3 duplicate removals · outlier flagging for prices >₹5,500 · 3-layer category mapping achieving 98.5% attribution accuracy.

Analytical Techniques Applied

1. ABC / Pareto Classification

Segmented 3,247 products by cumulative revenue contribution into A (top 20%), B (mid 29%), C (tail 51%) tiers for portfolio prioritization.

2. Coefficient of Variation (CV) Volatility Analysis
CV = (Std Dev of Daily Quantity / Mean Daily Quantity) × 100

Identified 746 high-volatility products (CV >25%) driving disproportionate inventory management costs.

3. Margin Proxy Modeling (P10 Heuristic)
Margin% ≈ 1 − P10(unit_price) / Avg(unit_price)

Flagged 869 products below 20% margin floor without access to actual COGS data.

4. Price Variance Index (PVI) + Shewhart X-MR Control Charts
PVI = Var(unit_price) / (Revenue / Quantity)

Detected pricing inconsistencies (36.6% average variance) and cashier-level overrides causing ₹14.4L annual leakage.

5. Dynamic Safety Stock Formula (ROP)
ROP = Mean_Demand × Lead_Time + Z × Demand_SD × √Lead_Time

Designed demand-responsive reorder points for 746 high-volatility SKUs, replacing static fixed reorder points.

6. Risk Stratification Matrix
Risk Score = (Volatility_CV% + Margin_Gap% + DSLS/10) / 3

Assigned RED/YELLOW/GREEN/BLUE zone labels to all 3,247 products for automated action triggers.

Key Findings

Five Quantified Business Problems

Each problem was quantified with financial impact, root cause identification, and an evidence-backed action plan.

Problem 01 · Most Impactful
Demand Volatility & Forecasting Gaps
₹31.7L annual cost
746 products (23% of portfolio) exhibit CV >25%. Average 3.2 stockouts + 2.1 overstock instances per SKU per month. Fixed reorder points unable to handle this level of variability — leading to simultaneous stockouts AND wastage.
Problem 02 · Portfolio Complexity
SKU Fragmentation & Long-Tail Inefficiency
2,595 SKUs → only 19% revenue
Classic 80/20 imbalance at extreme: 80% of SKUs contribute 19% of revenue. 97 products with >90 days zero sales. Results in procurement fragmentation, analysis paralysis, and ₹3.2L annual carrying cost.
Problem 03 · Profitability Crisis
Margin Compression — Below-Floor Products
₹22.4L/month at risk
869 products operate below 20% margin threshold. 95 products with negative estimated margin (pricing errors or untracked loss-leader bundling). Portfolio average margin 21.4% — dangerously close to floor with no guardrails.
Problem 04 · Revenue Integrity
Pricing Misalignment & Cashier Overrides
₹14.4L annual leakage
Top 20 volatile products show 36.6% average unit price variance. Cashier-level price overrides without audit trail. ASPARAGUS shows prices ranging ₹180–₹625 for the same product code. Customers confused; margins eroded.
Problem 05 · Enablement Priority
Category Mix Drift & Data Hygiene
40.28% revenue unclassified
Vegetables +42.84% growth vs. Fruits −20.61% over 6 months — but 40.28% of revenue (₹1.71L/month) tagged as "Unknown" category. Strategic blind spot: cannot make procurement or shelf-space decisions reliably.

Strategic Recommendations

Three-Pillar Optimization Framework

A sequenced, 12-week implementation roadmap targeting ₹72–100 lakhs annual net benefit.

🧹
Pillar 1 — SKU Portfolio Rationalization
Weeks 1–12 · Target: 3,247 → 1,800 SKUs (44% reduction)
🎯 Target: Revenue per SKU ₹7,818 → ₹14,108 (+80%) · Purchasing overhead −15%
  • Flag 200 lowest performers (<₹4K / 6 months) for 25–40% liquidation (Week 1–4)
  • Discontinue 600 Class C products with DSLS >90 days (Week 5–8)
  • Consolidate remaining 845 Class C products; test A/B bundling (Week 9–12)
  • Implement ongoing SKU performance review cadence (bi-weekly)
  • Financial Impact: ₹12–18L annual (carrying cost savings + freed working capital)
💰
Pillar 2 — Pricing & Margin Optimization
Weeks 3–8 · Target: Portfolio margin 21.4% → 23.0% (1.6pp lift)
🎯 Target: ₹28–42L from repricing + ₹14.4L from pricing standardization = ₹42.4–56.4L
  • Tier 1 (95 negative-margin SKUs): Immediate review and discontinue/reprice
  • Tier 2 (751 SKUs at 5–15% margin): +2–5% price adjustment with elasticity testing
  • Tier 3 (23 SKUs at 15–20% margin): Bundle with high-margin Class A leaders
  • Deploy centralized pricelist (daily CSV update to POS); flag >3% deviations
  • Mandatory manager override + audit trail for any price change >3%
📦
Pillar 3 — Dynamic Inventory Management
Weeks 4–8 · Target: Volatility costs ₹31.7L → ₹22.1L (−30%)
🎯 Target: Stockouts −68% · Overstock −24% · Wastage −60% · Turnover 23d → 18d
  • Stable (60% portfolio): Auto-replenishment with monthly review; 95% service level
  • Moderate (17% portfolio): Weekly dynamic ROP updates; 97% service level
  • High volatility (23% portfolio): Daily ROP recalculation using 30-day rolling demand
  • Slow-mover protocol: Flag day 30 → Discount 25–40% by day 35 → Discontinue day 60
  • Aging markdown automation: −20% at D-1; −40% in last 6 hours for perishables
